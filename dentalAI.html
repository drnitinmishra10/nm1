Abstract—
Dentistry has recently experienced an evolutionary growth phase in utilizing artificial intelligence (AI) in the early detection and treatment of dental diseases. In an on-going quest for maximizing patient care through an implementation of more accurate, efficient and effective dental practices, innovative technologies such as AI have garnered considerable interest among dental practitioners and associated oral healthcare teams. In this critical period for dental healthcare, AI technologies have come to the forefront to support dental practitioners in their daily tasks and challenge conventional diagnostic methods and their implementation by upgrading them with capabilities of processing large amounts of data and making precise decisions. This paper presents a comprehensive review of AI applications in dental disease detection, covering machine learning, deep learning, convolutional neural networks (CNNs), and clinical decision support systems, alongside the challenges of data privacy, algorithmic bias, and training requirements, and outlines a roadmap for responsible integration of AI into dental practice.
Keywords— Artificial Intelligence, Dental Diagnostics, Early Detection, Machine Learning, Deep Learning, Convolutional Neural Networks, Oral Cancer, Periodontal Disease, Dental Caries, Teledentistry.
I. Introduction

Dentistry has recently experienced an evolutionary growth phase in utilizing artificial intelligence (AI) in the early detection and treatment of dental diseases. In an on-going quest for maximizing patient care through an implementation of more accurate, efficient and effective dental practices, innovative technologies such as AI have garnered considerable interest among dental practitioners and associated oral healthcare teams. In this critical period for dental healthcare, AI technologies have come to the forefront to support dental practitioners in their daily tasks and challenge conventional diagnostic methods and their implementation by upgrading them with capabilities of processing large amounts of data and making precise decisions [1].

The significance of using AI in the early detection of dental diseases lies in the fact that it increases the accuracy and efficiency of the diagnostic process. Since machine learning algorithms are trained on large amounts of data, including dental images and patient data, they can identify tiny changes that humans cannot see. This helps in the early diagnosis of dental diseases, including caries, periodontal diseases, and oral cancer. They can develop in time before the first obvious clinical signs and symptoms appear.

The use of AI in dental radiography has become particularly important. Especially the application of image recognition in panoramic radiography can identify changes in the oral cavity that humans cannot recognize yet. By using CNNs, the results can be as accurate as those of experienced dentists [1]. This leads to a huge decrease in the number of missing diagnoses and allows for early treatment.

In addition to improving patient care, early detection and management of dental diseases utilizing AI can also lead to many positive changes in dental practices. With the incorporation of AI and predictive analysis into dental practices, there will be many opportunities for dentists to identify patients who are at risk of developing dental diseases and treat them before they develop. Practices that rely on AI are shifting from a reactive model of treatment to a more proactive model in order to provide the best dental care possible to their patients.

II. AI Technologies in Dental Diagnostics
A. Machine Learning for Caries Detection

The various applications of AI in dental diagnosis for the improvement of diagnostic accuracy have shown great promise. The use of machine learning for the detection and treatment of a variety of dental diseases has shown much promise. For example, many dental caries can be prevented by early intervention, by using enamel images to determine the extent of caries and the caries activity in intraoral photographs and in dental radiographs. A machine learning model has been designed to find the percentage of Dental Caries in a given dental image [8].

B. Deep Learning and CNNs

Deep learning has the ability to extract highly complex information within the data by the means of multiple layers of neural networks. In the field of dental diagnostics, the area of dental radiology greatly benefits from the applications of deep learning, particularly within the field of panoramic radiography image recognition [4]. The main purpose of these types of systems is to identify all types of dental anomalies found within two-dimensional images taken by means of panoramic radiography, including various stages of periodontal disease as well as peri-implantitis.

CNNs are frequently utilized in deep learning in order to analyze images and enable the system to recognize specific features that may have otherwise gone undetected. As a result, they are able to provide practitioners with highly accurate diagnostic results. According to Patil et al. [2], recent studies have proven that the latest diagnostic algorithms that employ deep learning are even more effective at reading dental radiographs than general dentists. Here, the complex algorithms are trained using thousands of annotated images.

C. Automated Analysis and Decision Support Systems

Automated analysis systems are one of the dental diagnostic tools which can assist the dental practitioner in the process of dental patient's data analysis and make the dental practitioner's work more efficient. By using a robust algorithm for patient-specific information and clinical data, the system can be programmed to give a patient-specific treatment plan. For example, an AI system can identify oral cancer in its early stage and suggest referral for further investigation and treatment. Decision support systems are also designed to work in conjunction with the dental practitioner's diagnostic capabilities in order to arrive at a correct dental diagnosis [8].

III. Applications and Case Studies
A. Oral Cancer Detection

The use of AI for the early detection of dental diseases has numerous areas of application. A very important and active area of research for the early detection of oral cancers, which if treated in time can increase patient's survival rate dramatically. For example, a study carried out by Nath et al. [14] which included in its research the use of AI, specifically neural networks for the analysis of images of patients' biopsies and their medical history. The researchers found that the use of AI for dental diagnosis increased the early detection rate of oral squamous cell carcinoma by 30% compared to dental visual inspection. In addition, the 5-year survival rate of this type of cancer went from 50% to 70%. This enables patients to receive the appropriate treatment in time and have a higher quality of life.

B. Periodontal Disease

The application of artificial intelligence in the early detection of periodontal diseases has also proven effective. The use of AI-based diagnostic systems that are capable of detecting periodontal disease from radiographs has shown promise. A recent study published by Messina et al. [15] reported on a number of case studies that highlighted the early detection and treatment of periodontal disease by using AI in dental screening. The study reported that the use of AI in screening for periodontal disease resulted in patients having an average of 25% reduced amount of time spent under treatment for the disease.

C. Predictive Analytics and Personalized Treatment

The use of AI for predictive analytics is transforming the way in which dental treatment can be managed. Dental treatments can now be offered on the basis of individual risk factors and the manner in which a patient is likely to react to a treatment. In a study conducted by Nath et al. [14], it was found that the use of AI for periodontal therapy led to the completion of successful treatment in patients, when measured by a reduction in the periodontal indices and an incidence of disease relapse. This could lead to the best form of treatment for a patient, meaning that there would be the best possible result from the dental treatment provided.

D. Economic Benefits

The use of artificial intelligence technologies in dental practices also can bring numerous economic benefits, including those which stem above all from a reduction of costs required for the diagnostic process of a patient. The data indicates that many of the procedures that are now a matter of course in dentistry could be performed automatically with the use of AI which would save time of a dentist and allow to reduce costs of implementation of these kinds of diagnostic procedures and thus implement them more frequently [15].

IV. Challenges and Limitations
A. Data Privacy and Security

While the advantages of using dental diagnostics with the aid of AI technologies offer much promise to the dental health care industry as a whole, there are several challenges which must be confronted by all health care providers before the technology is fully accepted into their practice. Perhaps the greatest challenge is data privacy and security. AI systems, no matter the form that they take, are developed using very large amounts of patient data which are gathered from many different sources. The protection of patient information is a must and health care providers must do their very best to safeguard all patient information in order to protect their patients from identity theft as well as data breaches and to protect health care providers from losing their licenses due to failure to comply with State and Federal Laws governing the use of patient data [1]. In the European Union and the EEA the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and in other parts of the world similar data protection laws are in place.

B. Training Data and Algorithmic Bias

The need for extensive training data is another aspect that must be tackled. AI algorithms are only as good as the data that is used for their training. In this sense, and when we speak of early detection of dental diseases, it is necessary to have a broad database that is able to be used by the algorithms in order to detect diseases in all kinds of patients, with different characteristics and different oral health conditions. Kolarkodi and Alotaibi [5] pointed out the lack of standardized data collection methods across the institutions, which makes it challenging to develop a single AI system that is able to be implemented in a variety of different settings.

There is a fear of in-built bias to AI programs. Algorithms can, on occasion, be biased in their decisions — often by default — particularly where there is a lack of representative data in the training set. Researchers, such as Alharbi and Alhasson [7], have undertaken systematic reviews of dental AI for early detection of dental diseases and have found that many of the algorithms used have varying levels of accuracy across different demographics, with particular concern for minority groups who are already disadvantaged in accessing healthcare.

C. Integration and Training of Dental Professionals

The training of all dental health care providers in the use of diagnostic AI will also be a challenge that must be overcome. Dental health care providers must be taught not only how to use the many different forms of AI diagnostics that are now available but also how to properly interpret all of the diagnostic information that is provided to them. There are a number of misconceptions within the dental community as to the application of AI in dental practice. A perception of 'black box' decision making by a machine needs to be allayed. Without proper education, patients and dental staff alike are unlikely to accept the increased use of AI in dental diagnostics [12].

V. Future Directions
A. Regulatory Frameworks and Ethics

The future of dental disease detection by means of artificial intelligence is to be based on a collaboration between health care professionals and IT specialists. Such a collaboration would ensure that newly developed diagnostic algorithms are based on a sound knowledge of dental pathology and are therefore to be used in a reliable and effective manner by health care professionals [9]. Regulatory bodies need to establish criteria and indicators that guarantee an adequate level of safety and effectiveness for dental diagnosis supported by artificial intelligence, also taking into account changes and technological advancements in this field.

As healthcare becomes more dependent on information that can be generated through use of technologies, questions have arisen about data collection, usage, and even whether certain diseases or conditions could be affected differently by AI-based systems than others. Of particular concern to dentists is the possibility that bias may be inserted into the system through data collection processes or even during development of the algorithms, leading to discrimination against certain groups of patients [20]. To counter these negative effects of AI in healthcare, new models of practice must be explored.

B. Dental Education and Curriculum Reform

Future generations of dental health care professionals need to be skilled in using AI for the early detection and diagnosis of a variety of dental diseases. For this to become a reality, there is a need for updated dental health care curricula. As Sarkar [12] stated, with the growing use of AI in health care and with a view to make dental health care informatics relevant to the current health care scenario, it is necessary to incorporate the concepts of dental health care informatics in the dental education programs. The educational programs will need to provide their students with sufficient knowledge about the algorithms that are used in various AI programs, and the ethical implications that are inherent to the use of AI in the health care domain.

C. Teledentistry and Real-Time Diagnostics

The future of the dental practice, incorporating the use of AI for early detection of diseases, could well change the way that patients are engaged in their own oral health. There is the development of real time diagnostics and teledentistry for remote patient consultations. The use of AI for real time diagnostic tools will change the nature of the clinician/patient interaction. There will be a requirement for dental professionals to be able to use these tools to inform their own diagnostic skills. There will be a requirement for secure data storage, patient privacy and the establishment of guidelines for the use of AI [3].

This further would enable implementation of a real time diagnostic aid for the dental health via mobile apps and telehealth and explore their potentials for patients' education and dental health monitoring. This in turn would aid in early diagnosis and timely dental interventions that could save patients from a lot of pain and suffering.

D. Interdisciplinary Collaboration

The dental health professionals and AI specialists will form a powerful combination, and the resultant collaboration will facilitate tremendous advances in the dental health care delivered to the patients. Dental professionals will guide the AI developers in the creation of the algorithms that will help in the accurate identification of a variety of diseases in their nascent stages. The current models of AI have not been able to create models that have the deep understanding of the dental health problems that the dental health professionals possess. Therefore, the interventional collaboration between the two groups will result in the development of clinically relevant and robust models [9].

VI. Discussion

While AI in dental diagnostics shows much promise, there are a number of challenges that need to be addressed. If these challenges are not met then the use of such health care technology could be compromised. In many instances, practices that rely on AI in their dental practices are shifting away from a reactive model of treatment and practice to a more proactive model in order to provide the best dental care possible to their patients. Patients are increasingly becoming more aware of the importance of health and as such, many are seeking the best methods of disease prevention [2].

In various regions, the processing time of dentist or dental staff members has decreased due to the introduction of AI enabled technology that processes the images quickly to reach a decision [15]. This in turn provides ample opportunity to dentists to engage in treating dental problems of their patients and improve overall quality of patient care provided. Workflow changes in dental practice can be initiated by the utilization of latest dental technologies which are used to enhance the oral health of patient and help dental practitioners in rendering quality dental care to the patients.

It is important to recognize the technology's place in the dental clinic and train health care providers to effectively use the technology, to enable the technology to be a tool to support precision dental medicine. Dentists must be aware of the current issues surrounding the ethical use of AI and work through them. Firstly, there is the sensitive handling of patients' personal information and the need to gain informed consent for the use of AI. Next, there are the inherent problems of algorithms and the lack of transparency and accountability in decision making. Most importantly, information from AI systems must be used in conjunction with the dentist's clinical knowledge and experience and not relied on in isolation.

A key challenge for development of robust dental disease detection AI is the need for ongoing research to improve the AI algorithms. High-quality data-sets that contain images of a wide variety of cases and that have been accurately annotated will be required for the continued development and training of the AI [7]. It is also expected that these systems will require on going updates to ensure the highest level of accuracy and to address any inherent biases. Addressing these challenges will require the involvement of a variety of stakeholders including researchers, AI experts, dentists and other ethicists and experts.

VII. Conclusion

In summary, even though AI has the potential of enabling novel and efficient diagnostics for dental diseases, there are a number of challenges, which need to be overcome before these systems can be implemented in a clinical setting. These challenges, mainly, center around data privacy, generation of large amounts of training data and the respective annotation as well as detection and minimization of possible bias within the respective AI algorithms.

As the integration of AI advances and is utilized more within the dental field, it will take on a pivotal role in the early detection and management of dental diseases and revolutionize dental practice. Continuous research into the technology, as well as health care providers and their patients being adequately trained and informed, will be crucial in ensuring that the many benefits of utilizing AI in the early detection of dental diseases are maximized while overcoming any current challenges faced within the industry.

The application of artificial intelligence in early detection of dental diseases signifies a new era in the dental healthcare practices and can potentially help in improving the accuracy of diagnosis and resulting patient treatment. To reap the benefits of AI in dental practice, it is imperative to ensure that this technology is used ethically, continuously updated and employed in collaborative environment amongst practitioners of varied disciplines. As long as these objectives are met, AI can prove to be a powerful tool in health care of mouth, not only for the dental practitioners but also for their patients.

The use of AI in the early detection of dental diseases will have far-reaching consequences that will create a dental health care revolution and lead to an increase in patient-centered dental health care, changing the clinical application of dental health care for good.
