Abstract
Natural Language Processing (NLP) has emerged as a transformative technology for sentiment analysis in e-commerce, enabling businesses to extract actionable insights from vast volumes of unstructured customer feedback. This paper examines the evolution of sentiment analysis methodologies from traditional keyword-based approaches to advanced deep learning architectures including BERT and CNN-LSTM hybrid models. We analyze the impact of NLP-driven sentiment analysis on consumer behavior, business strategies, and competitive intelligence in the e-commerce landscape. Through a comprehensive review of recent literature and case studies, we demonstrate how advanced NLP techniques improve sentiment classification accuracy, enable real-time business decision-making, and foster customer-centric product development. The paper also addresses critical challenges including multilingual processing, sarcasm detection, and data quality issues, while proposing future research directions in multimodal sentiment analysis and ethical AI frameworks.
Keywords—Natural Language Processing, Sentiment Analysis, E-Commerce, Deep Learning, BERT, CNN-LSTM, Consumer Behavior, Transformer Models

I. Introduction

Natural Language Processing (NLP) is today a key enabling technology for sentiment analysis. Most e-commerce companies receive large amounts of feedback from their customers, in the form of reviews posted online. Such user-generated content is influential for potential customers deciding whether or not to purchase from a particular company, and can have a direct and significant impact on a company's brand image and bottom line [1]. While companies have the ability to monitor such feedback on online channels, manually processing and analyzing large volumes of customer comments is time-consuming and costly, making automation an attractive option. The key challenge for sentiment analysis lies in leveraging NLP to transform the unstructured comments into actionable insights.

Reviews now contain more than just helpful information to potential customers; they contain slang, idioms, and even human emotions. Thankfully, natural language processing (NLP) technology has grown exponentially in capability to keep up with the rapid evolution of language in online reviews. NLP processes text through techniques like tokenization, sentiment scoring, and hierarchical deep learning models that help extract insights from human written language [2]. Some current NLP algorithms, especially those utilizing machine learning and deep learning, are able to go beyond positive/negative sentiment classification to provide a more in-depth look at customer emotions. By doing so, businesses can gain further insight into prevailing customer sentiment trends.

Feedback from consumers in e-commerce is a crucial element in evaluating products, services and sites. While helpful to potential customers for whom they express formal opinions, consumer feedback also can serve as an important feedback mechanism for a site and its products and services. By implementing Natural Language Processing (NLP) techniques on user generated content, sentiment analysis can be performed on reviews to determine if the user expressed positive, negative or neutral sentiment—such as excitement with a new product, frustration with a service or concern about pricing [3]. Sentiment analysis derived from NLP effectively can inform a site's business strategy while allowing the company to respond quickly to customer needs and sentiment.

Technological innovations, particularly the advent of transformer architectures in Natural Language Processing (NLP) like BERT and GPT have furthered the potential of sentiment analysis in e-commerce [4]. Different to prior approaches, these architectures process the full sequence of words, capturing contextual relationships and meaning. For retailers, this has enabled greater insight into products with dual sentiment, understanding customer concerns and the spectrum of motivations behind purchase decisions.

In addition to utilizing NLP for product reviews and sentiment analysis, e-commerce businesses can also leverage the technology for sentiment analysis of reviews of competitors in order to gain competitive intelligence. By identifying gaps in the market and gauging customer's likes and dislikes of competitors' offerings, a company can gain insights on how to develop their own products and implement more effective customer engagement strategies [5]. Companies can use the insights garnered from review data on their competitors' products to make predictions about future trends in order to develop a predictive strategy for business growth.

II. Evolution of Sentiment Analysis Methodologies

A. Traditional Approaches

Sentiment analysis has been traditionally done by techniques that rely on keyword spotting and rule based approaches. These approaches look for subjective words and patterns in the customer feedback and typically rely on sentiment dictionaries which classify words into positive, negative or neutral sentiments [6]. Language is complex and subjective, full of nuances such as sarcasm, while word semantics can evolve dramatically along the way they actually get used. Such simplistic approaches to processing human language can sometimes go astray. The ability to accurately determine sentiment in text is more complex and requires advanced approaches and techniques. Today, many businesses are interested in extracting meaningful insight from customer reviews. Simple, traditional sentiment analysis methods are often not sufficient to produce meaningful results.

B. Machine Learning Era

The advent of machine learning really kick-started the massive improvements in sentiment analysis in recent years, as Support Vector Machines (SVM) and Naive Bayes in particular became popular choices for text classification [7]. Feature extraction techniques, initially simple things like Bag of Words and later more advanced TF-IDF, enabled models to learn from labelled datasets to classify sentiment. However there is still a long way to go in terms of truly extracting meaningful semantic meaning from text. The ability to accurately classify sentiment for certain text where context or irony are crucial is currently a difficult problem.

C. Deep Learning and Transformer Revolution

Sentiment analysis has experienced a paradigm shift with the advent of deep learning, most recently embodied in the transformer networks such as BERT (Bidirectional Encoder Representations from Transformers) [8]. BERT outperformed all other methods at sentiment analysis, and its key advantage lies in its ability to understand the context of a word relative to all other words in a given sentence. Businesses will be able to extract more sophisticated insights of customer sentiment and subsequently use these insights to better segment customers on emotional dimensions and craft marketing strategies accordingly.

State-of-the-art models such as BERT not only achieve state-of-the-art results on sentiment prediction tasks, but also enable the use of transfer learning, where knowledge acquired for one task can be reused on another task [9]. In the booming field of e-commerce, with a constant influx of new products and developments, the ability to remain relevant to current sentiment is crucial for businesses to keep pace with shifting market trends and consumer behavior.

In addition to identifying positive, negative, or neutral sentiment, the advent of advanced sentiment analysis has also enabled the development of sophisticated sentiment scoring and sentiment visualization tools. Building on the back of deep neural networks, such models are able to output multi-dimensional scores, capturing complexity of sentiment that goes beyond simple binary classifications [10]. Businesses can tap into such insights granularly, triggering real-time shifts in strategy and reactions to trends in sentiment and feedback from consumers.

III. Hybrid Deep Learning Architectures

A. CNN-LSTM Hybrid Models

With every company turning customer-centric, accuracy and understanding of the content becomes a critical parameter for the business owners. To make right decisions while formulating product development strategy, revamping existing marketing plan or building new customer engagement model, it becomes indispensable to extract right insight out of customer reviews [11]. Using traditional method of keyword-based approach would not suffice, rather machine learning methods such as deep learning will prove to be helpful in analysis of customer sentiment and thereby giving edge to competitors over the market scenario. Sentiment analysis using Natural Language Processing techniques has brought revolution in e-commerce era.

Convolutional Neural Networks (CNN) combined with the recurrent nature of Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) networks form a good hybrid architecture in developing models for the classification of customer sentiments with increased accuracy and effectiveness [12]. These NLP techniques incorporate intricate nature of human language to enhance the contextual as well as feature extraction capabilities to derive the accurate sentiments carried out in the reviews.

B. Case Study: E-Commerce Review Classification

Hybrid models have found numerous applications in recent e-commerce case studies. In one such study, El Koufi et al. used a CNN-LSTM hybrid model to classify customer reviews on e-commerce websites and platforms using deep neural networks [13]. The results revealed an accuracy rate of 92% compared to the baseline models used in previous studies that reached an accuracy rate of 75%. The CNN model proved efficient in extracting spatial features from text while the LSTM network was effective in understanding sequential data. Such hybrid model enabled a more precise classification of customer reviews and aided in extracting sentiments expressed subtly in customer reviews. This is particularly challenging for simple keyword matching or lexicon-based methods.

In addition to the aforementioned application, Godia and Tiwari deployed a similar architecture (CNN-LSTM) to a large-scale dataset comprising more than 100,000 customer reviews, achieving very competitive results both in terms of accuracy and efficiency [14]. The obtained results demonstrated an approximately 40% reduction in the time required for analysis, which translates into e-commerce gain in terms of immediacy in responding to customers. This gain translates into gaining momentum in strategies, in optimizing promotional actions, in product development, and in fine-tuning customer service, all of which must be done in real time.

IV. Impact on Consumer Behavior and Business Strategy

A. Sentiment and Purchase Decisions

In addition to providing accuracy in the classification of product and brand sentiment, the innovative use of advanced NLP in this report also enables a nuanced understanding of consumer behaviour. By analysing customer sentiment at a granular level, retailers can gain deeper insights into preferences and trends influencing future buying behaviour, and apply these insights to inform key business strategies [15]. Retailers can use this information to drive marketing activity, inform stocking decisions based on trending sentiment, and enhance customer experience through more effective communication. Additionally, by identifying specific product features that receive positive or negative sentiment, retailers can quickly and effectively design targeted marketing campaigns or make swift changes to products or marketing copy to negate negative sentiment.

Reviews also contain emotional tone and underlying themes that hybrid models can determine. This enables businesses to understand not just the reasons for customers' sentiments, but also the underlying causes. Analyzing reviews using NLP therefore enables businesses to develop product and service improvements that are optimized to meet customer needs [16]. The use of NLP therefore offers businesses a competitive edge in a data-driven marketplace.

B. Correlation with Sales Conversion

When blending advanced NLP techniques with artificial neural network models, such as CNN-LSTM hybrid models, it becomes possible to achieve high-quality sentiment analysis outcomes in the e-commerce industry. By classifying and analysing customer sentiment, businesses can drastically change their customers' behaviour and make informed business decisions that can help an organisation stay ahead of the market and forge strong customer loyalty [17]. Sentiment analysis, powered by advanced natural language processing (NLP) techniques, is revolutionising the way in which businesses interpret customer feedback, and their customers' behaviour affected by this feedback. There are numerous studies that demonstrate the profound implications of sentiment interpretations on purchasing behaviour. Both favourable and unfavourable reviews have been shown to have a positive impact on consumer trust and behaviour, i.e., increasing or decreasing customers' loyalty to a particular brand. The research paper, Analysis of sentiment in e-commerce, by Ganguly and Solanki found that consumers tend to gravitate towards products and services that receive positive reviews online [18]. Building on these findings, the study found a strong correlation between positive sentiment and increased sales conversion rates. This means that an increase of 1% in positive sentiment in reviews could result in a 0.5% increase in sales conversion rates for a particular product.

Whereas positive sentiment can foster trust in consumers, negative sentiment can undermine it. Research has shown that one bad review is enough to make consumers less trusting. In fact, a 3-star rating can alienate up to 30% of potential customers, viewing the product as vague or mediocre, and inclined to opt for a 5-star rated alternative [19]. In a world where fast decision-making prevails and information is scant, the way in which we process information, or heuristics, can influence the extent to which we rely on review sentiment when making judgments about a product and, consequently, the extent to which perceived review sentiment affects consumer trust of a brand.

C. Customer Retention and Brand Loyalty

Granularity in sentiment analysis allows businesses to form a more informed marketing strategy. Identifying anomalies in the sentiment allows businesses to quickly determine if there is a problem with product quality or customer service. Sentiment analysis can also be used proactively to capitalise on customer interactions [20]. Ganguly and Solanki also found that 76% of respondents are likely to revisit a brand that addresses the customer's concerns highlighted in negative reviews in a meaningful way. As NLP evolves, businesses are beginning to leverage it for reaping benefits of turning a negative customer experience into a moment of reconnection with the customer.

In addition to providing organizations with a level of insight into historical customer behavior and attitudes, sentiment analysis has also proven to be effective as a predictive tool. Research has shown that the sentiment scores generated by natural language processing algorithms are strongly correlated with future purchasing behavior, indicating that they can be used to forecast sales trends [21]. Specifically, Taneja et al. discovered that the scores for a particular product yielded by NLP-powered sentiment analysis correlated at a .8x or higher with subsequent sales, demonstrating that organizations that implement this technology can use it for more than just looking back at past trends—they can also make predictions about future ones. By using analytics to make predictions about sales based on scores generated by sentiment analysis, businesses can more effectively manage their product inventory and create marketing campaigns that better reflect the sentiment of their customers, ultimately giving them an edge in the marketplace.

V. Competitive Intelligence and Market Trends

How do customer reviews influence the feeling on the market? Sentiment in language, or in NLP terms – binary – influences both regular consumer purchases and larger market trends. Analysing reviews left by customers provides valuable insight into the Zeitgeist of a brand [22]. A particular product may receive glowingly positive reviews that evoke an aspirational product image – the so-called halo effect, which then carries over to other categories of products and creates a lasting impression of the brand amongst customers. In this sense sentiment analysis creates a feedback loop that not only influences purchasing decisions in the immediate future but also influences loyalty and repeat business in the long run.

The level of sophistication that is achieved by applying advanced NLP techniques to sentiment analysis is beginning to have a profound impact on how people behave as consumers and how businesses respond to them. Namely, being aware of key metrics in the sentiment data can positively impact consumer trust and even affect purchase decisions as people begin to choose companies based on the quality of interaction with their brand [23]. This in turn is now shaping loyalty to brands and will continue to do so within the emerging e-commerce economy. Sentiment analysis using NLP is fast becoming an important aspect of any e-commerce business and will continue to play a vital role in the developing market.

VI. Business Applications and Case Studies

A. Targeted Marketing Campaigns

Targeted marketing campaigns is one of the major affected applications by Sentiment Analysis. Products and services on e-commerce websites are analyzed by NLP algorithms to identify customers' sentiments expressed in review content. Where positive sentiment is found in reviews, marketers can target specific products to customers in future advertisements with targeted messaging. Where negative sentiment is found, businesses can respond quickly to poor reviews by highlighting good aspects of service, or address issues to turn a negative review into a positive one [24]. According to research by Shang et al., businesses that leverage sentiment analysis for these purposes see higher customer engagement and retention, which in turn leads to better sales outcomes.

B. Product Development and Innovation

While improving the overall marketing strategies is a key outcome of the sentiment analysis, it also helps businesses to make informed decisions during the product development phase. By identifying trends in consumers' sentiment, organizations can see where there is room for innovation or address certain gaps in their existing offerings [25]. For instance, if negative feedback is repeatedly expressing dissatisfaction with a specific feature of a product, a company may decide to focus on redesigning that feature or developing alternative features and services that better cater to the preferences of its customers. Integrating consumer insight into the product development process can make companies more customer-centric and increase the likelihood that innovations will satisfy consumer needs and desires.

C. Strategic Planning and Customer Retention

Incorporating of customer feedback into strategic planning is becoming more important for companies, which can benefit from implementing customer sentiment analysis. In today's competitive market, companies that actually harvest and analyze customer sentiment have the opportunity to be more relevant and better anticipate market trends and consumer preferences [26]. Moreover, by adopting a proactive stance, businesses can foster customer loyalty in a market where repeat purchase is becoming increasingly important for many retailers, especially with low barriers to shop at alternative e-commerce stores.

Analysis of the sentiment outcomes can also give e-commerce businesses insights into how to develop successful retention strategies. Understanding how customer satisfaction is impacted by specific aspects of the experience enables businesses to design retention policies that effectively tap into the levers that drive high levels of satisfaction [27]. For example, if sentiment analysis reveals that timely delivery is a key driver of positive sentiment, a fashion business might highlight this benefit in its advertising and marketing campaigns, streamline its logistics operation to meet delivery commitments, or offer customers who select 2 day or next day delivery free upgrades or other incentives to choose these faster services. From both a narrative and brand positioning perspective, businesses can craft a coherent message about their value proposition by leveraging insights gained from analysis of sentiment outcomes.

VII. Case Studies in E-Commerce Implementation

Several case studies on using sentiment analysis in e-commerce illustrate the practical impact of NLP-driven insights. Bukhari and Sofiyan provided an illustration of a mature online retailer that analysed customer feedback posted on various platforms [28]. The analysis was done to find out consumers' perception towards the different variations of product and experience encounter offered by the online retailer. Natural language processing (NLP) technique was used in extracting and measuring sentiment from the online reviews. Results showed negative sentiment towards one of the products offered and thus informed the online retailer to re-evaluate the way products were managed and marketed. A strategic decision was later made to reduce the stock of the said product and concentrate on products that enjoyed high demand among consumers. The change not only improved customer satisfaction, but it also improved the way online retailer operated.

Moreover, Aydoğan and Okay focused on a leading e-commerce company that utilized sentiment analysis in producing product recommendations for customers [29]. Sentiment extracted from social media messages and product reviews was fed to the algorithm that identifies and analyses consumer preferences and buying behaviour. The study introduced a practical approach employed in the algorithm to utilise positive sentiment indicators in producing relevant product recommendations that maximise customer engagement and drive conversions. The case study details how accurate sentiment insights impacted the advertisement strategies of the company and how they reorganised their campaigns across different products to promote those products and content that received high scores of positive sentiments in the face of fluctuating tastes and preferences and achieved a significant increase in sales.

A well-known online fashion retailer used sentiment analysis to inform their customer service strategy after analysing post-purchase feedback. Using NLP techniques to identify patterns of frustration with their return process within customer reviews, the retailer found comments such as 'It was a bit harder to find what I was looking for' and 'I wasn't aware I could return items in any clothing store'. The retailer made changes to the website interface to improve communication with customers and clarified their return policy in the product description [30]. As a result, the business saw a marked increase in customer retention and brand loyalty, demonstrating the importance of sentiment data in understanding customer behaviour.

In addition, the e-commerce company learned that they could continuously monitor and track sentiment trends around sustainable products through customer feedback. This allowed them to shift their product assortment and marketing efforts to include more eco-friendly items, which helped them to connect better with this new segment of consumers comprised of ethically-minded consumers [31]. Real-time sentiment analysis is key in this new e-commerce landscape as it allows companies to better serve customers and retain their market share as trends continue to evolve.

VIII. Challenges and Limitations

A. Language Diversity and Multilingual Processing

Successfully integrating NLP in sentiment analysis in e-commerce customer reviews is challenging due to many reasons that need to be addressed in order to truly affect human behavior and decision making in online retail. One of the studied cases showed that language diversity in e-commerce is one of the major hurdles in achieving the said goal [32]. Since e-commerce websites are geographically diverse and are written in different languages and dialects, there is a great chance for misinterpretation. The existing approaches for sentiment analysis were mainly based on English language and lack the ability to generalize well to other languages; especially those with complex grammar rules and rich semantic resources. As it has been shown in Haroon et al., lack of sufficient training data in other languages is one of the main challenges that hinders NLP tasks including sentiment analysis from achieving desired accuracy levels to support e-commerce owners' benefit in order to extract insights from the customers' feedback.

B. Sarcasm and Contextual Nuances

There also remains a significant challenge for NLP systems in detecting sarcasm in customer reviews, particularly those that draw upon context, tone, and cultural knowledge. Sentiment analysis, which is already an imperfectly successful task for NLP, can easily be ineffectual in detecting sarcasm lurking behind the veneer of positive language [33]. Wang et al. argue that currently, 'most commercial and open-source sentiment analysis models' are unable to effectively recognise sarcastic language and comments within customer reviews, rendering existing NLP technologies ineffectual for informing key business decisions. In order to move forward in this area, there is a pressing need for NLP systems that are more capable of dealing with context, emotion and complex linguistic structures.

C. Data Quality and Noise

In addition to technological and operational challenges, there are additional obstacles that must be faced when leveraging sentiment analysis to gain competitive insights in the e-commerce industry. Many reviews, including star-based ratings, follow different patterns of words and length. Moreover, user-generated content contains a lot of noise, such as misspellings and slang [34]. Reviews can be written in casual language, which is generally more challenging to process for sentiment analysis algorithms. Furthermore, biased or even fake reviews are increasingly common and can negatively impact the results of a well-designed sentiment analysis solution if not treated appropriately.

D. Proposed Solutions and Future Directions

To deal with current problems in using NLP for processing customer reviews, there is a need for better and more comprehensive approaches. Sentiment analysis methods need to become more language independent to process reviews in any language or dialect [35]. Also, they need to incorporate a more contextual view, to deal with certain forms of language that people use to be sarcastic or use metaphors to express a sentiment. As it stands, these kinds of language are often misclassified by current sentiment analysis models. There is also a need to better handle low quality data, and how to perform adequate data cleaning. Some of the advanced techniques to consider in data cleaning include active learning, which allows a model to learn from a constantly changing set of data.

Feasibility of effective sentiment analysis tools for e-commerce still remains an open challenge to address, including the difficulties of handling negative reviews, dealing with spontaneous spoken words, capturing word choice variations, incorporating real-time processing, selecting relevant reviews, and managing high annotation costs [36]. By acknowledging these obstacles, the need for more research and industry applications becomes apparent, as NLP is challenged to keep up with e-commerce's rapidly evolving landscape. Moreover, as global e-commerce continues to boom, the challenge for effective sentiment analysis includes catering to the varied linguistic and cultural makeup of consumers. For natural language processing (NLP) to improve sentiment analysis in e-commerce, it must move beyond current implementations and adopt more advanced techniques to understand the complexities of human language.

IX. Advanced NLP Capabilities and Real-Time Processing

By enabling companies to better understand consumer behavior, NLP-powered sentiment analysis can form a vital link between a brand and its customers in today's digital commerce market. Analyzing unstructured customer reviews with NLP-based sentiment analysis gives businesses a way to accurately assess consumer sentiment, which can guide their product development, marketing, and overall customer service initiatives [37].

Unlike standard approaches to sentiment analysis, NLP brings a new level of nuance to understanding language. Rather than simply categorizing customer reviews as 'Positive' or 'Negative', using advanced techniques like contextual embeddings (BERT, GPT etc.) it's possible to understand the sentiment behind the text at a deeper level [38]. By acknowledging irony, sarcasm, nuances of language and the full range of human emotions, businesses can engage with their customers in a more informed way, going beyond a simple star rating and getting to the heart of what customers really need from them. It's a clear difference and a big impact. Understanding sentiment to drive customer loyalty, brand reputation and in the end sales.

Another significant benefit of using NLP in sentiment analysis is the real-time processing of the review data, so businesses can respond to their customers on the spot, and address any negative sentiments immediately [39]. The trends also enable businesses to adjust their marketing tactics in order to better satisfy their customers and increase customer retention. By analyzing customer feedback in real time, e-commerce businesses can engage with their customers and provide them with offers and promotions that are relevant to their feedback, thus increasing customer loyalty and revenue. The trends provide stakeholders with deep insights into the sentiments behind customer reviews and guide decisions made on inventory management, product development, customer service, and more.

X. Future Research Directions

Future developments of sentiment analysis will be an active area of research. In addition to the current focus on fine-grained sentiment analysis using natural language processing and data analysis techniques and methods, there will be growing interest in multimodal sentiment analysis that combines NLP with visual data analysis [40]. By analyzing customer uploaded images along with their written reviews, companies will be able to gain deeper insights into positive and negative sentiments and develop product preferences. There will also be continued innovation in machine learning and deep learning techniques and applications to analyze sentiment with greater accuracy and higher speed, to better enable companies to keep pace with shifting consumer behavior.

Sentiment analysis will incorporate more than just advances in technology, as consideration of ethics becomes increasingly relevant in this area of research. As sentiment analysis becomes more prevalent in various contexts, protecting the privacy of consumers and the safeguarding of their information will become a central research goal [41]. In addition, the development of frameworks that guide the use of NLP for sentiment analysis will become a prominent area of research as companies must strike a balance between their interests and those of their consumers.

Advances in Natural Language Processing (NLP) for sentiment analysis is transforming the way strategies for e-commerce and consumer behavior evolve. As businesses turn to more advanced methods for analyzing customer sentiment, implications for generic marketing strategies, product development and engaging with customers continue to expand [42]. Ongoing research is also driving the capabilities of NLP as well as addressing the ethical challenges of this important field, paving the way for the continued progression of sentiment analysis in aiding business growth.

XI. Conclusion

This paper has examined the transformative impact of Natural Language Processing techniques, particularly sentiment analysis, on e-commerce business models. Consumer insights generated from applying sentiment analysis enable e-commerce marketers to create more effective marketing campaigns, inform product development, and apply customer feedback to strategic business plans as e-commerce businesses compete for market share and sustainability. The studied cases showed a great positive impact on the customers' behavior and on business decisions derived from the sentiment analysis.

The following examples illustrate how the sentiment analysis can benefit the e-commerce stakeholders to gain deeper insights into customers' behavior and preferences, to analyze opinions and to adjust the business policies accordingly [43]. Successfully integrating NLP in sentiment analysis in e-commerce customer reviews remains challenging due to language diversity, sarcasm detection, and data quality issues. However, by acknowledging these obstacles and pursuing advanced techniques including multimodal analysis and ethical AI frameworks, the field continues to evolve toward more accurate, contextual, and actionable sentiment understanding.

As NLP technology advances, businesses that effectively harness sentiment analysis will maintain competitive advantage through enhanced customer understanding, predictive analytics, and real-time strategic adaptation. The convergence of deep learning architectures, transformer models, and hybrid approaches positions sentiment analysis as an indispensable tool in the modern e-commerce landscape, fundamentally reshaping how organizations connect with and respond to their customers.
