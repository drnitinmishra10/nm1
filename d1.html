AI in Effective Diagnosis of Diseases

Accuracy, Precision, and Recall Based on Published AI Diagnostic Studies

Important Note: The performance values below are representative ranges reported across AI-based diagnostic studies and reviews. Accuracy, precision, and recall may vary depending on dataset size, image quality, disease stage, algorithm, training method, and external validation.
Sr. No. Disease / Area Common AI Methods Reported Accuracy Reported Precision Reported Recall / Sensitivity Remarks
1 Cancer Diagnosis CNN, deep learning, radiomics, pathology AI, omics-based ML 85%–99% 82%–98% 80%–98% AI is widely used in breast, lung, skin, colon, and prostate cancer diagnosis using imaging and pathology data.
2 Diabetic Retinopathy CNN, fundus image analysis, transfer learning 90%–98% 88%–97% 87%–98% One of the strongest AI diagnostic areas due to large retinal image datasets.
3 Breast Cancer CNN, SVM, ANN, radiomics, mammography AI 90%–99% 88%–98% 86%–98% AI supports mammography, ultrasound, and histopathology-based diagnosis.
4 Lung Diseases CNN, X-ray AI, CT-based deep learning 85%–98% 83%–97% 82%–97% Used for pneumonia, tuberculosis, COVID-19, COPD, and lung cancer detection.
5 Heart Diseases Random Forest, SVM, ANN, XGBoost, ECG deep learning 85%–100% 84%–98% 82%–98% Some experimental datasets report very high accuracy, but clinical validation is still important.
6 Parkinson’s Disease SVM, Random Forest, CNN, voice analysis, gait analysis 78%–96% 80%–95% 78%–96% Recent review reported AI accuracy ranging from 78% to 96%; one multimodal framework achieved 94.2% accuracy.
7 Mental Health Disorders SVM, Random Forest, NLP, chatbot analytics, wearable AI 75%–95% 72%–94% 70%–94% AI is used for depression, anxiety, suicide-risk prediction, and monitoring, but datasets are often heterogeneous.
8 Sepsis Detection ML prediction models, EHR analytics, time-series models 80%–95% 75%–92% 78%–94% AI predicts sepsis risk using ICU data, vitals, lab values, and electronic health records.
9 Retinal Diseases CNN, OCT analysis, fundus image AI 88%–98% 85%–97% 85%–98% AI is useful for diabetic retinopathy, AMD, retinopathy of prematurity, and retinal vascular diseases.
10 Neurological Disorders MRI deep learning, EEG analysis, SVM, CNN 80%–97% 78%–96% 78%–96% Used for Alzheimer’s disease, epilepsy, stroke, Parkinson’s disease, and brain tumor classification.
11 Skin Diseases CNN, dermoscopy image analysis, transfer learning 85%–98% 83%–97% 82%–97% AI is widely applied in melanoma and skin lesion classification.
12 Infectious Diseases CNN, ML classifiers, outbreak prediction models 82%–98% 80%–96% 80%–97% Used for COVID-19, tuberculosis, influenza, Zika, Ebola, and other infectious disease detection.
13 Gastrointestinal Diseases Endoscopy AI, CNN, computer vision 85%–98% 84%–97% 83%–97% AI helps detect colon polyps, adenomas, colorectal cancer, and gastrointestinal lesions.
14 Kidney Diseases Random Forest, ANN, SVM, clinical ML models 90%–99% 88%–98% 87%–98% Chronic kidney disease prediction often shows high performance on structured clinical datasets.
15 Liver Diseases SVM, Random Forest, ANN, imaging AI 85%–97% 82%–96% 80%–96% AI supports diagnosis of fatty liver, hepatitis, cirrhosis, and liver cancer.
16 Oral and Dental Diseases CNN, dental radiograph analysis, image segmentation 85%–97% 82%–96% 80%–96% AI is used for dental caries, periodontal disease, oral cancer, and jaw abnormality detection.
17 Pediatric Diseases Clinical ML, imaging AI, decision-support systems 80%–95% 78%–93% 78%–94% AI is used in pediatric pneumonia, neonatal disease, congenital conditions, and pediatric imaging.
18 Rare Diseases Genomic AI, NLP, phenotype matching, clinical ML 75%–95% 72%–94% 70%–94% Performance depends heavily on availability of genetic and phenotype datasets.
19 Clinical Laboratory Diagnosis ML on biomarkers, blood tests, laboratory records 85%–98% 82%–97% 82%–97% AI improves interpretation of blood reports, biomarkers, and laboratory-based disease prediction.
20 Personalized Disease Prediction Predictive ML, EHR analytics, risk scoring, ensemble models 80%–96% 78%–95% 78%–95% AI predicts disease risk, disease progression, complications, treatment response, readmission, and mortality.

Key Interpretation

  • Highest reported performance: breast cancer, diabetic retinopathy, kidney disease, Parkinson’s disease, and heart disease prediction.
  • Most clinically mature areas: radiology, ophthalmology, oncology, cardiology, and pathology.
  • Most variable performance: mental health, rare diseases, and personalized prediction, because of heterogeneous data and subjective clinical labels.
  • Common limitation: many AI models show high accuracy on benchmark datasets but require external validation before clinical use.
